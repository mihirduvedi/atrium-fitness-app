What changed, and why it reads human

  • No glow, anywhere. The readiness ring is matte in both modes — Oura's rings never glow either.
  • Green is gone. Readiness, checks, and charts moved to dusty blue — the hue Oura itself uses for readiness. Positive deltas are blue; coral marks PRs and watch-outs.
  • Ink chrome. Buttons, the FAB, and your chat bubbles are warm ink (Notion's exact #37352F by day), inverted to warm off-white at night. No branded button color exists.
  • Warm text, never pure. No #000, no #FFF. The night canvas is warm graphite — your pick from the specimen round — under warm off-white text, so day and night share one paper-like temperature.
  • Native body type. Body text is your phone's system font, so the app feels like it belongs on the device. The serif appears only where the coach speaks — greetings, headlines, reviews.
  • Hero numbers set light. Big stats are thin and large like Oura's; small data rows keep Ledger's mono.
